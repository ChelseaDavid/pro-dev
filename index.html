    Weekly Reflections

    Week 1

    The main thing I have learned this week would have to be patience. Whether it be learning
    data design, UI/UX, or DOM, I feel I have really started to figure out the "just breathe" moto, and
    adapt to my learning styles. I have my work cut
    out for me, but as long as I apply the necessary tools, and remember to breathe, I believe I be successful
    at the end of this bootcamp. Though, I can now proudly say, I am familiar with basic HTML layout and format

    Week 2

    This week, for me, felt wildly more in depth than previous week. This week we were able to take a deep dive
    look into methods, accessors, mutator, and state variables, along with a few mySQL commands, and constructors.
    I am feeling fairly confident in the knowledge retention I have finishing out this week. Though there can be some
    uncomfortable moments I am happy to say I was able to adapt and overcome while still staying on track.

    Week 3

    This week, so far, has been the greatest week yet for me. This week challenged me to no end in PHP and
    some CSS, but all in all I feel i kept up well and will just need to review a bit over the weekend.
    We also got the chance to submit our capstone ideas and one of my ideas was selected for capstone!
    Still pretty stoked about that! Following the election for capstone ideas, we were broken into our groups
    for capstone and I honestly could not be more enthusiastic about my teammates if I tried. We all seem
    to work very well together thus far and all seem to have wonderful ideas for the direction we would like to
    take for our capstone. TL;DR This week was a great and challenging week!

    Week 4

    This week has felt like a real ramp up from previous weeks. The accessor and mutators are really starting
    to make sense for me. Still have some work to do fine tunning but overall feeling confident in the
    understanding I am gaining is so rewarding. It really makes the hard work seem worth it rather than feeling
    like i am spinning my tires in the mud getting nowhere. Also, this week has been a first for serious team development
    we are starting to see the real strengths and weakness in ourselves when applied to Capstone, and doing our
    best to tackle any pressing weakness as they arise while simultaneously building each other up when we can

    Week 5

    This week was a lot more challenging team wise than previous weeks. My team works well together with the
    exception of one particular team mate. I will not name names, but will just say the challenges of working
    with other people is draining on my patience this week. The lack of personal motivation and absent mindedness
    are really starting to become a problem. With a limited amount of time to complete this bootcamp, I am going
    to take this weeks challenge as a learning experience for myself, rather than waste time feeling negative about it.
    There will always be difficult people to work with in the workplace and this, like other things in the bootcamp,
    is just another situation of practice that will allow me to further better myself in a future work environment.

    Week 6

    This week was a nice breather. I was able to get somewhat ahead with my group and our capstone project. This
    week we were able to burn our first tickets and start writing our API's for each of our classes. Along with
    writing our API's we were also able to begin testing of those API's through an application called Insomnia.
    Testing is going well thus far and seems to be less chaotic and frustrating as unit testing. With all of that
    momentum and productivity, It allowed for me to feel as though i could take a breath when I get home each night
    rather than feeling like I have to rush onto the computer and shove as much information into my brain as the
    night will allow. It was also extremely informational attending the One Million Cups networking event. I was
    able to, with confidence, relay who I am, my goals, and purpose while simultaneously gaining potential
    clients, employers, continued education and professional development. TL;DR this week was fantastically rewarding
    and a boost in confidence.

    Week 7

    This week my group made some significant progress on our capstone project. We were able to burn our API
    tickets and begin working on configuring Angular properly. Each week seems to be one step closer and closer to
    not wondering around aimlessly in the desert of confusion. Also, George has been so helpful this week with both our
    group and each of us individually. This week I also had my phase 2b (half way) check-in on my PWP. Received
    some great feedback from george and was able to begin applying some of the suggestions to my page while simultaneously
    correcting a few errors I was receiving when running my page through the W3C validator. I was also able to
    begin preparing for our mock interviews coming up on the 31. I am very lucky to have help both in class and at home
    and fully intend to take any and all inputs I am given. TL;DR Our groups capstone is really starting to come together
    and PWP is coming along nicely.

    Week 8

    This week was extremely stressful for me. I was out with a pinched nerve and locked body for the majority
    of the week. Being stuck in bed and feeling as though I am falling behind. With the stress of interview day
    approaching, I was in full blown panic trying to study as much as I could in between trying to give my body
    rest. I don't do well when I feel unprepared going into any situation so missing 2 days this week really took
    it's toll. That being said, my entire class, instructors included, were incredibly patient with me this week
    and tried to help in any they could. This week was also interview week with both technical and HR interviews
    All in all I feel both interviews went very well. I received minimal critiques and positive feedback in both
    interviews. TL;DR This week sucked but I am grateful to my entire class for their help this week.
